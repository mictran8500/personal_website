About Me

Hi, I'm Michael.

I am currently on the final stretch of obtaining my Bachelor's in Computer Science (expected graduation: early-2022). I've been working as a QA specialist on my team for about half a year now. I believe my detail oriented mindset and strive for creating bug-free, user-friendly products makes me suited for a position like this. However, I can't help but feel like I'm not allowing my skills to flourish. My work in QA has given me the opportunity to get a close look at some amazing features implemented by the developers on my team. The software and technologies that they use are intriguing, and I only have admiration for their problem-solving skills and quick-wittedness when it comes to dealing with the defects and bugs I submit. That is why I've made the decision to put myself in a position where I can, to put simply, create.

I've had a ton of fun learning new technologies. My experience ranges from experimentation with AI algorithms, to database creation and management, to web development. Genetic Algorithms for Solving Sudoku Puzzles

The use of genetic algorithms have proven successful for a number of unique applications. Domains that utilize GAs range from game theory equilibrium resolution, to artificial creativity. GAs have also found success in solving numerous NP-complete problems, such as the Traveling Salesman Problem. While all NP-complete problems are equally difficult in regards to general computational complexity, some problems offer a much more advantageous representation for GAs. Thus, allowing GAs to be more effective in solving that specific set of problems. Our goal was to determine if Sudoku falls within that set.
